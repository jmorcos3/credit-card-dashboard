Which Marriott card: Brilliant vs Bevy? (The Math)

Points-only comparison:
• Brilliant: $650 fee ÷ 200,000 pts = 0.33¢ per point
• Bevy: $250 fee ÷ 175,000 pts = 0.14¢ per point

On pure cost-per-point, the Bevy wins — you pay 57% less per point. You get 87% of the bonus for 38% of the fee.

But factor in perks (Year 1):
• Brilliant: $650 fee - $300 dining credit - ~$400 free night cert - Platinum Elite = net cost ~$0 for 200K pts
• Bevy: $250 fee - ~$250 free night cert = net cost ~$0 for 175K pts

Bottom line: Both cards effectively pay for themselves in Year 1. The Brilliant is worth it only if you'll actually use the $300 dining credit and value Platinum Elite (10+ Marriott nights/year = room upgrades, lounge, late checkout). If you just want the bonus points and won't use those perks, the Bevy is the smarter play — same net cost, almost the same points, and $400 less risk if you cancel after Year 1.

Why never transfer Amex MR or Chase UR to Marriott?

Your Amex MR and Chase UR are worth ~2.0¢ each when transferred to airlines. If you transfer them to Marriott instead, they drop to ~0.7¢ each — you'd lose 65% of their value. Example: 100K MR = a $2,000 Delta One flight to Europe via Virgin Atlantic, OR just $700 in Marriott hotel nights. Always earn Marriott points from Marriott card bonuses and Bilt, and save your premium currencies for flights.
